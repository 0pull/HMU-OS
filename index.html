OS

Welcome to HMU-OS, an operating system that redefines the meaning of the word "meaningless." Designed with no clear purpose or functionality, HMU-OS is the epitome of digital mediocrity. Its interface is a monotonous black canvas, devoid of icons, menus, or any interactivity. The system does not support applications, file management, or even basic tasks such as timing. In fact, HMU-OS doesn't even have a clock. It just exists, consuming resources and doing absolutely nothing of value.

HMU-OS has a revolutionary "standby mode" that is constantly on, ensuring that users never have to worry about productivity or engagement. The only "trick" of the system is the blinking cursor in the upper-left corner of the screen, which only serves to remind you that the system is actually running. There are no updates, no security fixes, no support — because, frankly, there is nothing to support.

HMU-OS is ideal for those who like to stare at the screen and reflect on the futility of existence. It's not just an operating system; it's a philosophical statement about the meaninglessness of technology.

---

**Minimum system requirements:**

- Processor: 1 MHz (single core, no multitasking)

- RAM size: 16 bytes (yes, bytes)

- Memory capacity: 1 KB (for the entire operating system)

- Display: 1-bit monochrome (purple or off)

- Input: None (keyboard and mouse are not supported)

- Output: No (no sound, no visual effects except blinking cursor)

- Power: One potato (optional)
